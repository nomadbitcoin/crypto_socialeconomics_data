on 18 Feb 2018 at block 1371111, with a 10:1 Claim Ratio. For every 1 LTC held at the fork block, LTC holders could claim 10 LCC. If you held LTC at block 1371111 but haven\'t claimed yet, your LCC are still waiting for you!</p>\n<p>After the fork block, Litecoin Cash switched to SHA256 proof-of-work hashes. This enables a new use for previously obsolete Bitcoin mining hardware. Mining difficulty is recalculated every block, using Evan Duffield\'s proven DarkGravity V3 for more predictable block times as well as network protection from multipool hopping. To prevent early dominant miners from having an unfair advantage, block rewards scaled up linearly over the first 2000 blocks.</p>\n<p>The fork features replay attack protection &amp; a new address prefix to prevent confusion and inter-chain issues.</p>\n<p>LCC\'s target block time of 2.5 minutes gives 4 times the transaction bandwidth of Bitcoin, while transactions are 90% cheaper than Litecoin. It\'s centered around the concept of <span>worker bees</span>, which can be created by any LCC holder, who then becomes a <span>beekeeper</span>.</p>\n<p>Once mature, worker bees have a finite lifespan during which they live inside the blockchain and help to secure it by minting blocks. Beekeepers will earn rewards whenever one of their bees mints a block.</p>\n<ul>\n<li><strong>Existing SHA256 miners and pools are not affected.</strong> Proof-of-work blocks are accepted by the network at any time, so SHA256 mining hardware never needs to stand idle.</li>\n<li><strong>51% network attacks are rendered ineffective.</strong> Worker bees won\'t mint blocks on an attacker\'s private chain, so they cannot accumulate enough chain work to perform a 51% hashpower attack.</li>\n<li><strong>The mining process will be democratised,</strong> as any LCC holder can become a beekeeper.</li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<div class="row">\n<div class="col-lg-6">\n</div>\n<div class="col-lg-6">\n<div class="text" data-aos="fade-right">\n<h4>Prefer to learn by playing?
